Fossils are the preserved remains or traces of ancient organisms that lived millions of years ago. They serve as time capsules, helping scientists understand how life evolved and how the Earth has changed over time.

Fossilization is a rare process that occurs when an organism is buried quickly under sediment, protecting it from decay. Over time, minerals replace organic material or fill empty spaces, creating a rock-like replica of the original organism.

  • Petrification: Minerals replace organic tissues, forming stone-like fossils.
  • Cast and Mold Fossils: Impressions of the organism are preserved in rock layers.
  • Amber Fossils: Small organisms like insects get trapped in tree resin that hardens into amber.
  • Trace Fossils: Footprints, burrows, or droppings that reveal animal behavior.
